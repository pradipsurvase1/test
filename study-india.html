    Eligibility Criteria for MBBS, BAMS, BDS, BHMS and Medical PG in India
  • MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery): students must have to passed Class 12 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology subjects and secured a minimum qualifying percentile in the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET-UG). The minimum qualifying percentile varies depending on the category (general, reserved etc.).
  • Minimum age requirement is generally 17 years old as of December 31st of the admission year.
    • BDS (Bachelor of Dental Surgery):
  • Students should passed class 12 with PCB subjects and a qualifying percentile in NEET-UG is mandatory.
  • Minimum age requirement is typically 17 years old.
    • BAMS (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery):
  • Students must have to passed in class 12 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) subjects.
  • The entrance exam requirement might vary depending on the university. Some universities conduct their own entrance exams, while others might accept NEET scores. Minimum qualifying marks might also differ.
  • Age criteria might also vary slightly depending on the university.
    • BAMS (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery):
  • Students should Pass in BAMS in class 12 with PCB subjects is required.
  • The entrance exam is compulsory required and it should be differ by university. Some universities might accept NEET scores, or others should have their own entrance exams.
  • The age should be 17 years old to take the admission
    • Medical PG Courses (Postgraduate):
  • Requires an MBBS degree for admission.
  • Entrance exams like NEET-PG is compulsory for most Medical PG programs.
